About me

Me

I am a passionate programmer who is in his third year of university specializing in data science, and a robotics enthusiast . I love to create a positive impact within my community, whether it be through volunteering, clubs, or raising awareness on various topics that I am passionate about.

Team 188

This picture captures me and my robotics team, the 188 Blizzards, at the Detroit world championships in 2018, my final year in high school. I joined robotics during my first year of high school, and grew a great passion for it. This is what initially inspired me to pursue a career in computer science.

Why I volunteer?

The main reason why I volunteer is because I love giving back to the community. I especially enjoy volunteering with Robotics, because I love seeing the passion of stem within children. When I was younger, I did not have the same opportunities, and I feel as though I am giving back to the community as a volunteer, empowering the future of stem and giving them the opportunity I did not have.

Covid

Covid has really affected all of us in our own ways. For me covid really made me think about how the future was like before I was fully invested in my laptop as I believed in the future I would need something I can use primarily remotely. But covid has changed that for many of us, now working from home is the new normal, and I am really grateful that I have such a space at home.

ACM-W

I am so proud to say that I am a member of the ACM-W student chapter at OntarioTechU, where our goal is to raise awareness about the discrepancy of women in the stem field, and to empower those who are in these fields. Being male, and the dominant gender in this field, I feel we need to take accountability and bring equality for women; only then can we see real change. ACM-W has given me the chance to talk to fellow peers and professionals and understand what they go through, and I am grateful to be a member of this group.

Mentor

Starting university also meant that I had to say goodbye to my high school robotics team, the 188 Blizzards. In order to be actively involved with robotics after high school I decided to become a mentor. I love to educate other students about programming, but also educating them about stem. It really warms my heart to see the team and its individuals grow and become the success they are.
